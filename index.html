Culinary Gentrification: The Rise of High End Eateries in Brooklyn

By Ligea Alexander

August 6, 2023

This story is an attempt at capturing the nuanced transformation of a neighborhood, not just through overt signs of gentrification, but through the subtler shifts in culinary culture and business establishments. Gastronomy Alley spotlights the microcosm of this transformation in a single street, using it as a lens to explore broader socio-economic changes

I aimed to map the culinary and cultural metamorphosis of a Brooklyn street, using it as a microcosm to explore the subtle yet profound waves of urban gentrification and socio-economic transition.

Nestled between old-school juices and soulful seafood, marble countertops and negronis hint at a neighborhood in flux.

In a bid to keep pace with the changing times, long-standing shops are donning the unmistakable cloak of modern minimalism, merging seamlessly with the new wave of establishments.

Traditional businesses, not to be left behind in the march of time, have embraced the contemporary allure of understated design, blending effortlessly into the emerging landscape.
